Affiliation

What is ESC Affiliation?

ESC affiliation is a process through which engineering student organizations at Berkeley demonstrate their commitment to diversity, equity, and inclusion within their organizations. All of our affiliated organizations have attended several mandatory trainings, stripped exclusive requirements from their applications, and provided ESC with feedback from their members regarding the state of club culture at Berkeley. In return, ESC provides these clubs with a wide range of benefits, including access to funding and publicity. Next year, ESC is looking to partner with the College of Engineering and expand this list of benefits, creating an even greater incentive for clubs to join this program.

Why are we doing this?

For years ESC has received complaints from students that the club culture at Berkeley is too exclusive and unwelcoming, a description that fundamentally opposes our understanding of the purpose of student organizations. As beneficiaries of campus resources paid for by tuition dollars, we believe that all clubs (including ourselves) have a responsibility to serve the student body. The purpose of ESC Affiliation is to reward clubs who are already fulfilling this role and entice other clubs to do the same.

The Process

We put our clubs through a rigorous process to assure they are meeting our standards. Take a look at our process

Sexual Violence & Harassment Training

Why
This training enables club leaders to effectively respond and handle sexual violence and harassement training.

Leadership Training

Why
This training teaches skills essential to a strong leader. Club leaders should reflect on their leadership style and how it impacts their organization.

Equitable Strategies Training

Why
Student leaders need to learn how to seriously consider how they can promote equality in their application process and club.

No GPA minimums / transcripts / resumes

Why
Clubs should admit students based on interest and demonstrated commitment to the organization rather than prior experience.

Demographic Survey

Why
This enables ESC to better understand the demographic of students who are joining our affiliated organizations.

Our Affiliated Clubs

The Engineering Student Council at Berkeley has over 60 affiliated student organizations, including competition teams, honor societies, and community outreach groups. All of these clubs have enjoyed incredible success in their fields, and they have each committed to promoting an environment of diversity and inclusion both in their club and in the broader engineering community. Finance

ESC awards funding to our affiliated organizations through Initial Allocations (spring) and Ficomm funding (periodically throughout the year). We generally have around $70,000 to award annually, so we encourage all of our affiliated clubs to apply!

Treasure Training

All of our affiliated organizations are required to send their treasurers to one of our Treasurer Training every year, the purpose of which is to educate clubs on how they can apply for and spend funding granted by ESC. Due to the complexity of this training, we have included the slides on our website for the convenience of our affiliated clubs.
