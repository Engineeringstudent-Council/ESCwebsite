Housing Help

This guide was created to help incoming/exisiting students find affordable housing in Berkeley. While this guide does not have a housing portal, we do offer available housing portals that cover the Berkeley area. If any of the information is incorrect, please email us at technical@esc.berkeley.edu

First Thoughts

When first looking for housing, it can seem a bit daunting to find a place. Understandably for many, this is the first experience they will have to find housing in Berkeley. As a first step, look for roommates. Since there are many different types of housing, having an agreement with your roommates is key to having a smooth living accommodation process. Once you have roommates, remember to keep calm! Finding housing can be hard especially when trying to work with other people. Compromises may need to be made but losing your calm should not be one of them.

Get Started

Step 1: Roomates
Find people you want to live with, communication is key (1-3 roomates recommended)

Step 2: Rooms
Studio: 2 people max, 1 Bedroom: 2-3 People max (3 is a tight fit), 2 Bedroom: 3-5 People max (5 people is a tight fit), 3+ Bedroom: Usally 2 people per bedroom

Step 3: Distance
Near Campus (5-10 min walk): Places close to campus are usally 1 Bedroom/Studio apartments
Close but not near campus: Usally have 2-3 bedroom apartments avalible
3 is rare in new buildings unless there is a penthouse

Step 4: Cost
Set a limit to how much each person would want to pay

Step 5: Necessities
Prepare a list of potential roomate things you guys would:
a) Absolutely NEED
b) To make it comfortable
c) Would be nice to have

Step 6: Ask Around
Ask upperclassmen friends (perferably graduating) if they're leaving their place

Step 7: BME
Check out Bane of my Existence spreadsheet:
a) For questions to ask during a tour
b) Rental agencies with properties near campus

Step 8: Facebook Pages
Free & For Sale (cheap furniture / apartment necesities)
Berkeley Off-Campus housing page (can also post for help in finding apartments)

Step 9: Spreadsheet
Copy BME spreadsheet to your own Google Drive
Catalog any places you've seen

Tips & Tricks
People who are vacating their apartment usually give a 90 day notice to their rental company (usually March 1st)
Start finding roommates early (December/January)
Do not worry if you cannot find a unit by early April, rental companies and property owners will be posting rental listings until July (at the earliest)

Using the Bane of My Existence guide

This guide has been been tested and shared for the past four years. What started as one engineer's struggle to find housing transpired to compiling housing agencies that serve the Berkely area and questions to ask while going through a housing tour.
